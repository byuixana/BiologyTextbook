Term Description

*Below is a table of common terms that you will find in your studies of anatomy this semester. Sometimes these terms will be combined in a way to give a structure more meaning. We recommend that you review this list and commit the descriptions to memory. Later when you learn more specific anatomical structures, these terms will help you.

Finally, notice that when you hover on some of the more tricky sounding words, you will see a speaker icon to the left of them. If you click on these speaker icons, it will play the audio of someone saying the term to help you pronounce some of the more difficult words.

Prefixes and Suffixes
a- or an- Without or lacking (ex: atheist meaning "without" belief in a deity)
ab- away from (ex: abnormal meaning "away from" being normal
bi- and di- two (ex: biceps or two heads of a muscle)
epi- upon, above or on top of (ex: epicondyle of the humerus - meaning above the condyle of the humerus)
-oid Resembling or to be like (ex: Rheumatoid or to be like Rheumatism...and rheumatism means joint pain)
-osity To be full of or abounding in (ex. curiosity means to abound in or be full of curiousness)
semi- Half of (ex; semiannual is at the half year)
